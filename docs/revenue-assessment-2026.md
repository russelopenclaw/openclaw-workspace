### Marketing (Simple, Non-Sleazy):

**#1: Just Start Talking to People**
- Friends, family, local businesses you know
- "Hey, I'm offering automation services. Know anyone who might benefit?"
- Word of mouth > ads

**#2: Document Your Work**
- Twitter/LinkedIn: "Built this automation for a client. Saved them 10 hrs/week."
- Don't sell, just show what you did
- People will ask "Can you do this for me?"

**#3: Free Tier / Trial**
- "30-day free trial. If it doesn't save you 5 hours, cancel. No hard feelings."
- Removes risk for client
- Confident in your work? This is easy

**#4: Niche Communities**
- Reddit (r/smallbusiness, specific industry subs)
- Facebook groups for local businesses
- LinkedIn groups in your niche
- **Don't spam** — provide value, mention your service when relevant

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Underpricing** — Don't compete on price. Compete on quality.  
❌ **Scope creep** — Define exactly what's included. Charge for extras.  
❌ **No contracts** — Use simple service agreements (even with friends)  
❌ **Chasing shiny objects** — Pick one, master it, then expand  
❌ **Perfectionism** — Ship ugly but functional. Iterate later.  
❌ **Ignoring cash flow** — Get paid upfront or 50% deposit
